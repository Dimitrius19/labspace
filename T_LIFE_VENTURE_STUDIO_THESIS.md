## 1. What this is

T-Life Capital Venture Studio is the vehicle through which **Dimitri Tryfon** (4th-generation principal of the Tryfon family) builds **new operating businesses outside pharma**, deliberately diversifying away from family concentration in ELPEN / WinMedica / Tripoli HPAPI.

Every business produced by the studio is:

- **AI-native by construction.** AI agents perform every task that AI agents can perform. Humans are hired only where genuinely required. This is an operating-model commitment, not a marketing posture — it determines the cost structure and therefore the unit economics that the thesis competes on.
- **Externally led at the operating layer.** Each company onboards a recruited CEO (or operating GM) per company. Dimitri sits at the studio level — board, capital, network — not running the operations day-to-day.
- **Dimitri-majority-owned** at inception. Studio capital from T-Life Capital balance sheet. Outside capital (VC, family-office co-invest) only where the deal needs it — never as a default.
- **Outside pharma**, deliberately. MedAdherence-RWE (the pharma-adjacent buy-side play surviving in the parent catalog) is an outlier, not the template.

## 2. What it is NOT

The studio is **not**:

- A pharma adjacency roll-up (that's what T-Life Capital does separately around ELPEN/WinMedica/Tripoli — MedAdherence-RWE belongs to that lane).
- A VC fund. No external LPs, no diversified portfolio with predictable IRR distribution targets, no fund-life pressure.
- A consulting / services firm dressed up as a studio.
- An incubator for external founders without the studio principal in the cap table at majority.

## 4. Active sourcing scope (first cycle)

Three sector clusters in the first sourcing batch:

1. **Regulated knowledge work where AI eats billables.** Legal, accounting/tax, insurance brokerage, audit/assurance, IP filing. AI replaces 60-90% of billable-hour work in regulated professions where compliance is the moat the incumbent thinks they have but is actually automatable.
2. **Vertical B2B SaaS for under-served Greek + EU SMEs.** Construction trades, hospitality ops, marine services, agriculture supply chain, real estate property management, vet clinics, beauty/wellness chains. Big TAM, weak incumbents (or US-only), AI-native build can leapfrog without retrofitting.
3. **Government / public-sector AI automation.** Long sales cycles but huge contracts (€0.5-5M ACV). AI eats the services-firm consulting layer (Deloitte/EY/McKinsey public-sector practices). Sovereign procurement is a feature, not a bug — but candidates must not compete with sovereign-built free systems (the corporate Arachne / reBrainGreece kill pattern).

**Explicitly excluded** from the first cycle: pharma adjacencies (separately handled), consumer apps, social/networking, climate / ESG reporting (different operating muscle), pure-finance fintech (regulatory complexity disproportionate to studio scale at first cycle).

## 5. Methodology — every candidate must pre-clear

The 9-of-9 KILL rate on the prior cohort hardened the sourcing methodology. Every candidate proposed for the studio MUST pre-clear these filters BEFORE it enters serious diligence:

1. **Not a feature inside a system-of-record.** If a named SaaS incumbent could ship the proposed product as a module within 12 months, kill before proposing. Validate against the named incumbent's 2024-2026 product roadmap.
2. **Not a free-replacement sovereign system.** If a Greek/EU/member-state digital agency could supply this for free (corporate Arachne / reBrainGreece pattern), kill before proposing.
3. **AI-native at COST level, not AI-flavored.** The thesis must work because 1 AI agent does the work of 10 humans at the incumbent — 10× unit-cost advantage, not "uses LLMs." The incumbent's cost structure is the moat we are attacking.
4. **Defensible at STRUCTURE level.** Capital (acquisition target list), data (proprietary feed), regulatory (license/cert), relationships (named, hard-to-replicate). NOT tech moat alone.
5. **Named concrete buyers, not abstractions.** Each candidate names 3-5 reachable organizations with rough budget. "Greek SMEs" is not a buyer.
6. **Binary Phase 0 kill test ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks.** Failing the test kills the bet cleanly. No mushy "validate and learn" probes.

Every candidate also receives a 2-round adversarial red-team BEFORE Phase 0 funding. Round 1 happens at the candidate-list level; round 2 happens once the candidate has been re-spec'd against round 1's findings. The MedAdherence-RWE pattern (v1 → red-team → v2 → 2nd red-team → v3) is the standard, not the exception.

## 7. What we learned and what we changed

The 5 days of catalog iteration that produced this thesis are documented in `PORTFOLIO_RESHAPE.md` and `PHASE0.md`. The central methodology lessons:

- **The tactical filter is necessary but not sufficient.** "Infrastructure to incumbents" archived 25 obvious SOR-replacement plays correctly. It then passed 9 entries that all died under adversarial review. A filter that produces 0-for-9 on validation is a screening tool, not a portfolio shape.
- **Adversarial review is cheap; founder-recruit + Phase 0 burn is not.** Two rounds of red-team on each candidate cost ~€2-5 per round. They saved ~€500K-1M of Phase 0 burn on theses whose load-bearing assumptions were already empirically dead.
- **Defensibility comes from structure, not technology.** Every surviving thesis (only MedAdherence-RWE in the prior catalog) defended at the structure level: balance-sheet capital, family asset edge, regulatory know-how, named buyer warmth. No technology moats survived.
- **AI-native at unit-cost level is the actual edge.** Everything else (UX, integrations, regional adaptation) is a feature incumbents can ship. The one thing they cannot ship without rebuilding their company is their cost structure.

These four lessons inform every sourcing cycle going forward.

## 8. v2 — Lessons from Sourcing Batch 01 (added same day, 2026-05-21 night)

Sourcing batch 01 produced 11 candidates that pre-cleared the 6-filter brief. Round-1 red-team on the top 5 produced **4 KILL + 1 REVISE**.

Combined with the prior 9-of-9 filter-survivor kill rate, the cumulative outcome is **13 of 14 candidates dead under adversarial review**.

The patterns are now too consistent to be noise:

1. **"Big 4 won't cannibalize their billables" is empirically dead in mid-2026.** Killed StateAidOps + DG-Studies. Deloitte Zora is at $840M run-rate; EY.ai is shipping; PwC ChatPwC live. The 2026 reality: Big 4 are cannibalizing their pyramids faster than the conventional assumption predicted, driven by client cost pressure post-RRF + the AI labour-pricing reset.

2. **"Regulated mid-market buyer can't be reached by AI-native SaaS at this price band" is dead.** EpsilonNet ships PYLON Hybrid Agentic AI ERP into Greek accountants; OneTrust ships DORA at €1.5K/yr; Kickster has Piraeus office with 500+ vessels post-March 2026. Greek mid-market is not under-served.

3. **"Sovereigns won't build the recipient-side / commercial layer" requires per-case validation.** Held in Aktis (Ministry of Shipping has no remit). Died in StateAidOps (Italy ReGiS by Sogei + Greek OPS-TA are state-built) and Accountant-OS (AADE 2026 ships pre-filled VAT returns free).

4. **Public-list-based buyer segmentation is unreliable.** Aktis' "sub-30-vessel Piraeus owners" segment doesn't exist as a coherent buyer set — those owners cluster at 90+ vessels through related entities (Procopiou ~91, Thenamaris ~95). The "fragmented gap" assumption was a desk-research artefact.

5. **Speed of M&A consolidation closes wedges within months.** Localyze → Boundless Oct 2025 killed MobiVisa; Cegid + Shine Nov 2025 + Pennylane $200M Jan 2026 killed Accountant-OS; Entersoft + SoftOne → ENTERSOFTONE Oct 2025 closed the Greek accountant tail; Kpler + Spire + MarineTraffic + FleetMon consolidated the maritime data layer in 12 months. Sourcing methodologies cannot stay ahead of this cadence at quarterly cycles — they need to source at edges incumbents structurally CAN'T reach.

## 9. v2 — Broadened Studio Mandate

The studio mandate now formally includes three motions (any of which can be the first or second bet):

### Motion A — AI-native venture build (the original v1 mandate)
Build a new operating business from zero. Recruit external CEO. Phase 0 → seed → A. The MedAdherence-RWE-shape that produced one survivor in v1.

### Motion B — Acquisition-led PE-with-AI
Acquire an existing sub-€5M EBITDA operating business in a consolidating vertical, AI-overhaul operations to expand EBITDA 2-3× within 24 months. T-Life family balance sheet at €2-5M is structurally sized for this. The MedAdherence-RWE Phase 1 (tail-PIS acquisition) pattern broadened to other verticals.

### Motion C — Dimitri-runnable AI-native services firm
A small services firm where AI agents do 80%+ of the delivery work and Dimitri personally operates with 2-3 senior AI engineers + 0-2 domain experts on retainer. Year-1 revenue €300K-1.5M; off-balance-sheet from T-Life Capital; no VC. Cash-flow / lifestyle-PE shape rather than venture shape.

**Each motion has its own Phase 0 economics** and the studio reserves can flow to any of them based on conviction.

## 10. v2 — Sourcing Variants (Active)

Sourcing batch 02 (in flight as of 2026-05-21 night) targets three new sourcing variants under the broadened mandate:

- **Family-network-edge sourcing** — bets where the moat is a named senior relationship at the Greek/EU industrial-elite tier (TERNA, Mytilineos, PPC, Bird & Bird, magic-circle Brussels, named shipping families). If the named relationship is removed, the bet falls apart.

All three variants share the hardened 6-filter pre-screen + 2-round adversarial red-team methodology. Expected hit rate higher than 7-13% (the gap-analysis baseline) because each variant explicitly requires a structural edge that the generic sourcing did not.
