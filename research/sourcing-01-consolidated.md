## Pattern observations

**Where the agents agree.** The strongest candidates (Aktis, StateAidOps, Atlas, Accountant-OS) all share three structural features:
1. **Regulated-or-quasi-regulated buyer** with predictable budget and procurement path
2. **Incumbents are services firms** (Big 4 + magic circle + Greek law firms) charging €1,400-2,000/hr — AI replaces 60-80% of structured work at 1/10 cost without cannibalizing the incumbent's billable pyramid (incumbent won't ship the cannibalizing product)
3. **Defensibility from structure not tech** — capital float (Aktis), regulatory channel (Accountant-OS through licensed accountants), named-relationship moat (StateAidOps + family knows the prime contractors), Greek-bar admission (Astynomia)

**Where the methodology bites.** The "AI overlay to an existing SaaS SOR" pattern died across all three clusters — Toast/Square/ServiceTitan/Jobber + Veeva/CoCounsel/Wolters Kluwer + SAP/Oracle public-sector + gov.gr/AADE/IDIKA all ate the obvious plays. The candidates that survived target buyers the SOR vendors don't reach — Greek mid-tier accountants, Piraeus sub-30-vessel owners, named RRF prime contractors, vet independents resisting consolidation.

**What's notably absent.** No "AI agent for X" thesis where X is a generic SMB workflow. No "vertical SaaS for Y" where Y is restaurants/trades/retail. No "compliance automation" for use cases the Big 4 already bundle into audit. The agents pre-killed those.

## T-Life-asset-edge correlation

The three candidates with **HIGH** asset edge (Aktis, StateAidOps, Atlas/Accountant-OS at MEDIUM) all leverage things only this family has:
- **Aktis:** balance-sheet capacity to float €5-15M EUA inventory (no pure-SaaS competitor can do this) + Greek industrial credibility to unlock sub-30-vessel Piraeus owner meetings
- **StateAidOps:** TERNA / Mytilineos / PPC owner-level relationships (the Greek industrial elite is small and family-driven)
- **Atlas:** Greek bank board relationships
- **Accountant-OS:** mid-tier accountant credibility from 4-gen family business

The candidates with LOW edge (JudgeStack, Short-Let-OS) are venture-shaped but compete on pure product — those die first under adversarial review.
