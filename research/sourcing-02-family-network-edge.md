## 1. JCA-Native Dossier Studio — AI-built EU Joint Clinical Assessment dossiers as a service for mid-cap EU pharma

**The relationship (named):**
- Tier: Father's-generation peer pharma owners + retained magic-circle EMA counsel.
- Specific named seniors: Theodoros Tryfonas / ELPEN board peers at **Pharmathen** (Aposporidis family), **DEMO**, **Vianex** (Giannakopoulos), plus Dr. Anna Tryfon's EFPIA-side contacts; **Bird & Bird Brussels (life sciences partners)** and **Sidley Austin Brussels** who already advise ELPEN on EMA matters.
- How originated: 4-generation pharma family relationships through ELPEN/WinMedica; ELPHA peer board introductions; pre-existing retainer relationships with the law firms.
- What this senior can approve: A €150-300K paid Phase 1 JCA-dossier pilot, signed off by Pharmathen's Head of Regulatory or DEMO's CMO. Bird & Bird partner can refer 2-3 mid-cap EU pharma clients facing 2026-2027 JCA deadlines.

**The bet (without the relationship):**
EU HTA Regulation (EU) 2021/2282 went live Jan 2025 for oncology + ATMPs, expanding 2028 to all centrally-authorised meds. JCA Coordination Group projects 35 oncology + 15 ATMP + 5 high-risk JCAs in 2026 — a 3x volume jump. PICO complexity has been flagged by EFPIA as the #1 bottleneck. A normal SaaS startup selling "AI for regulatory submissions" gets buried under IQVIA, Parexel, ICON — none of which a mid-cap CRO can displace via LinkedIn outreach. Regulatory affairs is hired on prior-relationship trust; mid-cap pharma CROs do not buy from unbranded vendors. Cold-outreach conversion ~0.5%.

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:**
A JCA dossier currently costs €400K-€1.2M from a tier-1 CRO and runs 9-14 months with a team of 6-12 regulatory writers, biostatisticians, and HEOR specialists. An AI-native studio with one senior regulatory partner + agent fleet (PICO scenario generation, comparator literature mining, evidence synthesis, country-deviation drafting) can deliver in 10-14 weeks at €150-250K, with the human partner as the EMA-credible signing authority. Margin: 65-75% vs CRO 18-25%.

**Why incumbents can't replicate the relationship:**
IQVIA/Parexel sell from London/Basel and price for big-pharma. Mid-cap EU pharma (€200M-€2B revenue) is structurally underserved and buys from peers' recommendations. Bird & Bird partners refer regulatory work to firms they personally trust — that referral channel is not on a price list. ELPEN-family endorsement to Pharmathen/DEMO/Vianex CMOs is a single phone call; no PE-backed roll-up has that.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: At least 2 named mid-cap EU pharma CMOs (introduced by ELPEN board peers or Bird & Bird partner) will sign a paid LOI of ≥€100K for a JCA-dossier pilot on a real upcoming submission, conditional on a credible AI demo against one published EUnetHTA21 case.
- Experiment: Build one full mock JCA dossier (PICO matrix + comparator synthesis + country PICO deviation drafts) for a public oncology product using the EU HTA framework. Run the demo at 3 introductions arranged by Theodoros Tryfonas. ≤€50K total (1 senior regulatory contractor + compute).
- KillSignal: <2 paid LOIs after 3 demos; OR named senior declines to make introductions; OR Bird & Bird partner refuses referral.
- ValidateSignal: ≥2 signed paid LOIs ≥€100K each from named mid-caps.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.35

## 2. Construction-Concession Claims & Variation Engine — AI-native claims/variation drafting for Greek mega-concessions (BOAK, Attiki Odos extensions, North Road Axis)

**The relationship (named):**
- Tier: Owner-CEO / family-board at Greek listed construction.
- Specific named seniors: **Georgios Peristeris (GEK TERNA Chairman)**, **Dimitris Koutras (AKTOR CEO post-Reggeborgh)**, **Christos Panagiotopoulos (AVAX)**, plus partners at the Greek arms of the Brussels firms (Sidley / Arnold & Porter when EU-funds compliance overlaps).
- How originated: Tryfon family + ELPEN board overlap with the GEK TERNA / Mytilineos board ecosystem; same Athens club tier; existing Bird & Bird relationship covers EU public-procurement law.
- What this senior can approve: A €200-500K paid pilot for a single mega-project (e.g. BOAK Heraklion-Chania €2.05B concession awarded to GEK TERNA in 2026). Decision is owner-CEO level because claims/variations are a board-visible P&L lever.

**The bet (without the relationship):**
Greek construction backlog is €18.56B across the big four (GEK TERNA, AKTOR, AVAX, Metlen), with €8-10B in new PPP tenders pipelined over 18-24 months. On every mega-concession, 8-22% of final contract value migrates through variation orders and claims — currently drafted by Deloitte/EY/PwC + magic-circle outside counsel at €600-1200/hour per resource, with cycle times of 6-18 months per claim and material under-recovery (industry estimates: 30-50% of legitimate claims abandoned). A normal startup pitching "AI for construction claims" cannot get in the room — these decisions are made by the chairman, not a procurement function. No external software vendor has ever displaced Deloitte/EY at GEK TERNA. Cold conversion: 0%.

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:**
A typical FIDIC Yellow Book claim file is 800-3000 pages of dispatched-records, weather logs, RFI threads, RFI delays, BIM diffs, subcontractor notices. Current human teams take 4-8 weeks at €200-400K per claim. An AI-native studio with 2-3 senior FIDIC counsel + agent fleet (timeline reconstruction, EOT entitlement analysis, quantum modeling, precedent retrieval against ICC/DAB awards) delivers in 7-14 days at €40-80K per claim. The senior counsel signs. Repeat across a single concession's 40-120 lifetime claims = €5-12M revenue per concession.

**Why incumbents can't replicate the relationship:**
Deloitte/EY are conflicted (they audit the concession entities or the lenders), expensive, and slow. The Big 4 cannot drop their effective rate without cannibalizing the rest of the engagement. Construction-tech startups (Procore, Bentley) don't sell legal/quantum work. The chairman-level trust required to hand over claim strategy is generational — it took 30 years to build, and it sits in the Tryfon family's peer set. PE-backed roll-ups buy assets, not Athens dinners.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: At least one Greek listed contractor chairman (introduced by Tryfon family directly) will sign a €200K+ paid pilot on a single live claim file from BOAK, Attiki Odos extension, or a current PPP, conditional on a 7-day turnaround demo against a redacted historical claim.
- Experiment: Take one publicly-available ICC/DAB award + redacted FIDIC dispute file. Reconstruct the claim end-to-end via agent fleet + one senior FIDIC contractor. Demo to one chairman in a private meeting arranged by Dimitri's father. ≤€50K (senior counsel day-rate + compute).
- KillSignal: Chairman declines the meeting; OR meeting happens but no paid pilot offered; OR pilot offered but at <€100K (signal: treated as a curiosity, not a P&L lever).
- ValidateSignal: Signed paid pilot ≥€200K, with named project, named claim, and 30-day kickoff.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.30

## 3. Greek Shipping FuelEU/ETS Compliance Backbone — AI-native compliance + allowance-trading desk for named Greek family shipowners

**The relationship (named):**
- Tier: Owner-principals of named Greek shipping families.
- Specific named seniors: **George Procopiou (Dynacom / Sea Traders / Dynagas)**, **Maria Angelicoussis (Angelicoussis Group / Anangel)**, **Evangelos Marinakis (Capital Maritime)**, **Nikolas Vardinoyannis (Avin)**. Tryfon family overlap is Athens society + Vardinoyannis cross-tier (Vardinoyannis sits in both shipping + Motor Oil/HELLENiQ orbit, which is pharma-adjacent through hospital ownership).
- How originated: Greek industrial-family peer network; Vardinoyannis-Tryfon historical proximity through philanthropic boards and the Athens private-hospital ecosystem.
- What this principal can approve: A €300K-€800K paid pilot covering 8-30 vessels for a full FuelEU + EU-ETS-Maritime + CII compliance + allowance trading service, signed by the owner directly.

**The bet (without the relationship):**
EU ETS Maritime hits 100% surrender from 2027 (on 2026 emissions). FuelEU is live. A Greek dry-bulk-only fleet of 8 vessels faces a documented $4.1B lifetime compliance cost (study from sustainable-ships.org, 2026). Greek owners control ~21% of world tonnage. Currently NAPA, OceanScore, DNV Veracity, Marine Benchmark are selling SaaS dashboards at $200-500/vessel/month — the dashboards do not actually execute compliance or trade the allowances; that is still being done by chartering desks + brokers + outside counsel, badly. Cold outreach to Procopiou / Angelicoussis fails 100% of the time. Shipping is the most "you-know-someone-or-you-don't" sector in Europe. A normal SaaS startup will sell 2-3 small fleets at $300/vessel/month and die.

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:**
The actual job is not the dashboard, it is: (a) per-voyage emissions accounting, (b) FuelEU pooling strategy across the fleet, (c) ETS allowance forward purchasing + hedging, (d) CII rating optimization via routing/speed, (e) charterer pass-through clause negotiation, (f) regulatory filing. Currently done by ~4 people per 20-vessel fleet at the owner level, plus retained outside counsel and a broker. AI-native: one senior maritime CO2 trader + agent fleet replaces the bottom 3 of those 4 humans and the broker. Pricing: €2-4K/vessel/month managed-service (not SaaS) = €500K-€1.5M/year per family fleet, vs incumbent dashboards at €60K/year that don't actually do the trading.

**Why incumbents can't replicate the relationship:**
NAPA/OceanScore are software companies; they will not staff an allowance trading desk. Big 4 are conflicted (they audit the same families). Outside counsel cannot trade allowances. The trust required for a Greek principal to let an outside firm run his ETS allowance book is generational — only a peer family with skin in the game can earn it. A Geneva trading shop cannot get past the secretary at Angelicoussis.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: One named principal (target: a Procopiou or Vardinoyannis fleet, introduced by Tryfon family) will commit to a paid 90-day shadow-pilot on 5-10 vessels at ≥€100K, with the option to convert to managed service.
- Experiment: Build a working FuelEU pooling optimizer + ETS allowance simulator using public AIS + IMO data on the principal's own fleet (publicly known vessels). Present a counterfactual: "Your 2026 H1 compliance bill could have been X% lower." Single meeting arranged via family channel. ≤€50K (one ex-Trafigura/Vitol carbon trader contractor + compute).
- KillSignal: Principal declines the meeting; OR meeting happens but no shadow-pilot offered; OR offered but at <€50K and unpaid.
- ValidateSignal: Signed paid shadow-pilot ≥€100K with named vessels and 30-day data access.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.40
