# Acquisition-Shaped Candidate Theses — T-Life Capital AI-PE Motion

Each thesis pre-cleared against: not-already-rolled-up, not structurally compressing, named candidates visible, specific labor-replacement P&L, €2-5M scale fits family balance sheet.

## 1. AE-RollUp — Greek/Cypriot small architecture & engineering studios serving RRF / public-sector tender work

**Targets (GEMI / public-tender derived):**
- **Salfo & Associates** — Athens civil/transport eng., ~€4-7M rev, Salfopoulos family.
- **Doxiadis Associates** — Athens A&E, owner-managed, ~€3-5M rev, succession-exposed.
- **OMETE SA** — multidisciplinary, ~€3M rev, founder-led (Diavgeia awards).
- **GAIA SA** — geotech/environmental, ~€2-4M, owner-managed.
- **Roïkos SA** — Athens A&E, RRF-active, founder-controlled.
- **Cyprus**: Atkins-Cyprus alum spin-outs, 5-15 person studios via CCTE registry.

**Acquisition deal box:** €2-5M EV, 4-6× normalized EBITDA, typically equity + 24-mo earn-out tied to RRF backlog burn-down. Founder succession is the unlock — most owners are 58-68.

**Why now:**
- Greek RRF + ESPA 2021-27 biggest tender-driven A&E spend wave in 30 yrs; bigger studios win prime, sub-€5M losing share.
- AKAEM digital/BIM compliance mandate — small studios can't afford BIM seats + staff.
- EU CSRD + EPBD energy-modelling burden on every public retrofit.
- No PE roll-up yet visible in Greek A&E (Egis/Systra/Arcadis 2024-25 focused on Western EU).

**AI-overhaul P&L thesis:**
- Pre-overhaul typical: rev €3M, EBITDA €450K (15%), headcount 28 (4 principals, 8 senior eng., 10 CAD/BIM techs, 4 admin, 2 tender writers).
- Post-overhaul 24mo: rev €3.5M (capacity uplift on same principals), EBITDA €1.1-1.3M (32-37%), headcount 14.
- Labor going away: (1) **CAD/BIM drafters** 70% via text-to-BIM (Forma + RAG over KENAK); (2) **tender writing** 80% via LLM on Diavgeia corpus; (3) **energy compliance calcs** 90%; (4) **structural calc reports** 60% (engineer reviews, doesn't write); (5) **admin/coord** 50%.

**Why PE can't do this:** Egis/Sweco/Arcadis have unionised CAD/BIM staff + matrix cost structure — radical cut politically impossible. Greek PE (Diorama, EOS, Olympia) lacks AI muscle, prices for steady-state EBITDA.

**T-Life asset edge:**
- Tryfon family Greek public-sector relationships (pharma → Min. of Health, Min. of Development) translate to A&E tender introductions.
- ELPEN owns Greek industrial real estate — pilot client for the first acquired studio (captive demand).

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: ≥2 of the named targets engage paid scoping (≥€10K Phase 1 diligence retainer) at ≤€5M EV indicative with founder-succession openness.
- Experiment: Greek M&A boutique (Axia, Eurobank Equities, or Pelagos) mandated for €30K to run 12 targeted outreaches over 10 weeks; parallel build of AI-BIM/AI-tender-writing demo on synthetic Diavgeia data to show to engaged sellers.
- KillSignal: <2 engaged sellers at acceptable EV, OR demo shows BIM-text quality below pro-engineer acceptance threshold in 8-week build.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team): 0.45**

## 2. SpecialtyBroker — Greek/Cypriot specialty commercial-lines insurance brokers

**Targets (HAIB registry + GEMI):**
- **Matrix Insurance & Reinsurance Brokers** — Athens, commercial + marine, ~€3-5M rev, founder-controlled.
- **Karavias Underwriting Agency** — marine/cargo, family-owned, ~€2-3M.
- **HAIB top-50 tail**: 4-6 sub-scale brokers visibly squeezed post-Howden 2023 entry (names from 2024 HAIB rankings).
- **Cyprus**: Pittas Insurance, Limassol marine-cluster founder shops sub-€3M rev.
- **EuroInsurances Brokers** — ~€2M, owner-managed.

**Acquisition deal box:** €2-4M EV, 5-7× EBITDA or 1.0-1.5× commission revenue (standard broker comps). Earn-out tied to client retention 24mo.

**Why now:**
- Howden entered Greece 2023; Marsh McLennan + Gallagher scouting 2024-25.
- IDD-II + BoG capital requirements 2025 squeezing sub-€2M shops.
- Greek brokerage founders mostly 60+, succession-ready.

**AI-overhaul P&L thesis:**
- Pre-overhaul typical: rev €3M, EBITDA €600K (20%), headcount 22 (3 producers, 12 account servicers, 4 claims handlers, 3 admin).
- Post-overhaul 24mo: rev €3.6M (producer capacity unlock), EBITDA €1.4-1.6M (38-44%), headcount 11.
- Labor going away: (1) **account servicers** 70% (renewal questionnaires, COI issuance, endorsements against carrier portals); (2) **claims FNOL intake** 90%; (3) **commercial submission prep** 80% (LLM reads BOR letters + loss runs, drafts submissions); (4) **policy comparison/quote-mapping** 95%.

**Why PE can't do this:** Howden/Gallagher/Marsh acquire on retention covenants — can't fire account-servicer headcount yr 1 without breaching earn-outs. Bermuda/London cost structure assumes 15-20 servicers/producer; AI-native unlocks 40+ but only if owned + rebuilt day 1.

**T-Life asset edge:**
- ELPEN + WinMedica generate ~€2-4M annual pharma insurance spend (D&O, product liability, marine cargo, employee benefits) — captive flagship account anchor for acquired broker.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: ≥2 named brokers engage paid scoping at ≤€5M EV.
- Experiment: €25K to HAIB-connected M&A intermediary for 8-target outreach; €15K build of demo "AI account servicer" against synthetic carrier portals (Ergo, Generali Hellas, NN Hellas).
- KillSignal: <2 engaged at acceptable EV, OR carrier portal automation blocked by carrier T&Cs (regulatory diligence reveals API access requires master-broker status the acquired co lacks).

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team): 0.50**

## 3. IPBoutique — Greek/Cypriot/EU patent & trademark prosecution boutiques

**Targets (EPO/EUIPO rep lists):**
- **Patrinos & Kilimiris** — Athens IP, founder-aged, ~€2-3M rev.
- **Malamis & Malamis** — IP prosecution, family-controlled, ~€2M.
- **Vayanos Kostopoulos** — IP/tech boutique, ~€2-4M.
- **Tsibanoulis & Partners** — separable IP arm, ~€1.5-2M.
- **Cyprus**: Patrikios Pavlou + Chrysses Demetriades IP arm carve-outs.
- **EU tail**: 5-10 PT/PL IP boutiques sub-€3M (EPO rep registry).

**Acquisition deal box:** €2-4M EV, 4-6× EBITDA. Asset deal (book of recurring renewals = predictable annuity). Earn-out on renewal retention.

**Why now:**
- Marks & Clerk, Dennemeyer, Murgitroyd rolling up European IP boutiques 2023-25.
- EPO Unitary Patent (2023) compresses translation/validation revenue.
- EUIPO automated examination 2024-25 squeezing TM prosecution fees.
- Founders uniformly 60+ in Greek IP bar.

**AI-overhaul P&L thesis:**
- Pre-overhaul typical: rev €2.5M, EBITDA €500K (20%), headcount 18 (3 partners, 5 patent attorneys, 6 paralegals, 4 admin/renewals).
- Post-overhaul 24mo: rev €2.8M, EBITDA €1.1-1.3M (40-46%), headcount 9.
- Labor going away: (1) **paralegals on renewals + docketing** 85% (commodity work, trivially AI); (2) **TM search + clearance opinions** 70%; (3) **office-action response first drafts** 60% (attorney reviews); (4) **translation/validation admin** 90%.

**Why PE can't do this:** Marks & Clerk / Dennemeyer roll-ups preserve partner P&L share + paralegal headcount (partners refuse to cut leverage ratio — it's their economics). Bar regs require attorney *sign-off*, not attorney *drafting* — PE acquirers don't push this; owner-operators do.

**T-Life asset edge:**
- ELPEN + WinMedica IP portfolio (active pharma patents, ~80-150 active filings est.) — material captive book transfers in year 1.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: ≥2 named IP boutiques engage paid scoping at ≤€5M EV with successor-anxiety signals (founder >62, no internal partner-track successor).
- Experiment: €20K to legal-sector M&A advisor (e.g. Fairfield or Greek equivalent) for targeted outreach; €20K build of AI office-action-response demo on USPTO/EPO public corpus to validate partner acceptance.
- KillSignal: Greek Bar professional-conduct opinion against AI-drafted attorney work product, OR <2 engaged sellers.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team): 0.35**

## 4. PropMgmt — Greek/Cypriot mid-tier residential property management chains (post-Airbnb tail)

**Targets:**
- **Athens Blueground tail**: 5-10 100-500-unit serviced-apartment operators in AirDNA rankings (UPSTREET, Athens Quality Apartments, Sweet Inn Athens franchisee).
- **Cyprus**: Pafilia mgmt arm carve-out; Limassol Russian-portfolio managers post-2022 sanctions reshuffle.
- **Thessaloniki**: 3-5 mid-scale STR managers, founder-controlled, post-2024 tax-squeezed.

**Acquisition deal box:** €1.5-3M EV, 5-7× EBITDA. Asset-light (no real estate owned, just management contracts).

**Why now:**
- Greek 2024 STR tax + licensing squeezed amateurs → pros gain share, margin-compressed.
- Blueground/Sonder/Numa rolled up institutional tier; sub-200-unit operators in no-man's land.

**AI-overhaul P&L thesis:**
- Pre-overhaul: rev €2M (mgmt fees on €15M GBV), EBITDA €300K (15%), headcount 18 (1 GM, 4 ops coord, 6 guest-comms, 3 revenue/pricing, 4 cleaning coord).
- Post-overhaul 24mo: rev €2.4M (capacity for +30% units same headcount), EBITDA €700-900K (29-37%), headcount 9.
- Labor going away: (1) **24/7 multilingual guest comms** 85%; (2) **dynamic pricing** 95% (automatable, just not done); (3) **cleaning coord** 70% (agent dispatches gig cleaners); (4) **booking-platform reconciliation** 90%.

**Why PE can't do this:** Blueground/Sonder VC-funded growth-mode, won't cut CX/ops headcount (their brand promise). Local incumbents lack technical capacity.

**T-Life asset edge:** None material beyond capital + patience. Honest call.

**Phase 0 kill test (binary, ≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):**
- Hypothesis: ≥2 sub-€3M EV targets engage paid scoping with willing-seller signals.
- Experiment: €20K direct outreach via STR-industry consultant; €20K AI guest-comms demo (Greek/English/Russian/German) against synthetic Airbnb/Booking message corpus.
- KillSignal: post-2024 STR-tax reform has structurally compressed unit-economics below thesis (verify via 2024 P&L diligence on 2 targets).

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team): 0.30** (operational complexity + thin margins make this the weakest of the four; included only because it survives the gate, not because it's strongest.)
