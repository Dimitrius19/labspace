## 1. EU AI Act + DORA Conformity Filings for Greek Industrial Mid-Caps — AI agents read the regulation, map to client systems, produce the dossier; Dimitri signs as senior advisor; retained Greek attorney countersigns

**Buyers:** Mytilineos/Metlen (AI across energy + metallurgy), Titan Cement (AI risk in ops + HR), Viohalco (multi-entity — each subsidiary needs its own filing), Fourlis (HR AI triggers Annex III), Quest Holdings (DORA scope on financial-services arm).

**Deliverable:** Annual: AI Act gap assessment + Annex IV docs; DORA ICT-risk + third-party register; 30-page sign-off-ready board memo; one re-test cycle. No subscription.

**ACV:** €60-€140K/client/year; €180K Year-1 if discovery heavy.

**Year-1:** 6-8 × €90K = €540K-€720K. Aug-2026 AI Act deadlines force action; Big 4 quote €300K+; family-tier skips procurement.

**AI stack:** A1 ingests regulation + Commission guidance + client system inventory → gap matrix. A2 drafts Annex IV documentation from interviews + specs. A3 maintains third-party/ICT-asset register + quarterly diffs. A4 generates board memo. Human: 4 interviews/engagement (Dimitri), legal opinion (attorney ~6 hrs/filing), QC. AI 85-90%.

**Why Big 4 / boutique can't match cost:** Big 4 burn 400-700 manager hours at €280-€400/hr; pyramid can't be dismantled without breaking the audit business. They price €250-€400K for what costs Dimitri €12K of agent + 30 hours of partner+attorney. Greek boutique firms (Karatzas, Bernitsas, Zepos) won't scale — partners would draft personally, no associate leverage.

**Why Dimitri-runnable:** No associates, no PMs, no BD (8 callable names). Two AI engineers maintain stack; attorney on €8-12K monthly retainer batches sign-offs. Dimitri: 3 calls/week + Friday QC. Fits 50-60%.

**T-Life edge:** All 5 named groups in Tryfon industrial peer set; warm intro at board-secretary level gets the meeting within 2 weeks.

**Phase 0 (≤€50K, ≤16 wk):** Hypothesis: 2 of {Mytilineos, Titan, Viohalco, Fourlis, Quest} sign paid scoping ≥€25K by Wk 16. Wk 1-3 build stack + retain attorney; Wk 4-6 demo Annex IV dossier on a hypothetical Greek industrial AI system; Wk 7-12 deliver via 5 warm intros + quote €25K scoping; Wk 13-16 close 2. Cost: agents €18K + attorney 3-mo €30K + travel €2K = €50K. Kill: ≤1 paid scoping.

**p(red-team):** 0.50. Hits: (a) Deloitte/KPMG Greece productize aggressively, but their floor is €200K and senior-led boutique beats below; (b) AI Act timeline may slip — but DORA in force since Jan 2025 and CSRD digital tagging live, cross-sell carries.

**Founder-fit:** Yes. Dimitri read EU regulatory text voluntarily during catalog phase; product is "translate Brussels into board language for people I know." Risk: boredom by month 18 → scale or shut down. Enjoyment 7/10.

## 2. EU Innovation Fund + Horizon Europe Tender-Writing for Greek Industrial Bidders — "win-fee + retainer" proposal shop; AI agents draft 90% of the 80-200-page tender; Dimitri owns the strategic narrative

**Buyers:** Mytilineos (renewable + battery storage), Titan Cement (low-carbon cement, IF repeat bidder), Helleniq Energy (hydrogen + CCUS), PPC/DEH (grid + storage), Motor Oil Hellas (refining decarbonisation), Terna Energy. Second tier: Coral, Lamda, ElvalHalcor.

**Deliverable:** Per-tender: eligibility + scoring memo, full proposal draft (text, work packages, GANTT, budget, impact), two revision cycles. Pricing: €40-80K fixed + €150-400K success fee (0.5-1% of €30-80M tickets).

**ACV:** Fixed book €40-80K × 8-12 tenders = €450-€800K. Success fees 2-3 wins × €200K = €400-€600K (mostly Year 2).

**Year-1:** Cash €450K-€700K (fixed fees); Year-2 €800K-€1.4M including success fees.

**AI stack:** A1 ingests call docs + scoring rubric + client past proposals + Cordis winning-proposals → "winning shape" memo. A2 drafts proposal sections to rubric. A3 builds budget tables + consortium matrix. A4 maintains Greek-industrial reference library (each client's projects, KPIs) reusable across tenders. Human: SME interviews (Dimitri 2-3/tender), strategic narrative + "why us" section, final review. AI 85%+.

**Why Big 4 / boutique can't match cost:** PNO and Deloitte's EU-grants practice charge €120-€250K fixed on 600-1000 consultant-hours per proposal. Cost floor stays above €100K (pyramid + EU office overhead). €50-€70K with senior partner reviewing personally is structurally cheaper than incumbents can match without cannibalizing.

**Why Dimitri-runnable:** PNO has 600 employees across hundreds of low-margin proposals. Dimitri's version targets 8-12 high-quality proposals from 6 accounts where relationships pre-exist. No BD, no coordinator (AI does it). Two AI engineers + Dimitri + technical writer at €60/hr for surge.

**T-Life edge:** Awarded tenders involve 5-year oversight + reputational exposure. Family-network access removes the "do we trust this consultant with our 3-year R&D narrative" question that kills cold-pitched grant boutiques.

**Phase 0 (≤€50K, ≤16 wk):** Hypothesis: 1 paid tender ≥€40K AND 2 LOIs for 2026 H2 tenders by Wk 16. Wk 1-4 build stack against the public IF Round 5 call + demo proposal for synthetic Greek battery-storage project; Wk 5-10 demo to grant directors at Mytilineos, Titan, Helleniq, PPC, Motor Oil; Wk 11-16 close 1 paid. Cost: agents €20K + tech writer €10K + travel €5K + data €5K = €40K. Kill: 0 paid.

**p(red-team):** 0.45. Hits: (a) success fees concentrate in 2-3 binary outcomes/yr — fixed-fee book covers opex; (b) IF cycles may compress 2026-27 — RRF tail + Horizon + Cohesion compensate; (c) PNO will respond with AI tooling — likely, but salary base keeps cost floor high.

**Founder-fit:** Yes, with caveats. Dimitri likes strategic narratives; won't enjoy budget reconciliation (AI does it) or consortium herding. Enjoyment 6.5/10. Watch: if month 9 has Dimitri at 40% on consortium logistics rather than narrative + close, model failed — exit, don't hire ops.

## 3. Family-Office Investment Memos for Greek + Cypriot UHNW Family Offices — outsourced deal-screening + memo desk for the ~30 families doing €5-50M direct investments without a full in-house team

**Buyers:** Latsis (Paneuropean Holdings), Niarchos foundation deal arm, Demetra Holdings (Cyprus), Vardinoyannis (Motor Oil + media), Kontominas, Restis, Vassilakis (Avis Greece), Daskalantonakis (Grecotel). Cypriot tier: Loutsios, Christodoulou, Shacolas.

**Deliverable:** Annual retainer: weekly 1-page deal-flow note, 3-5 deep memos/year on shortlisted deals (15-25 pages: market, competitive map, financial model, downside, reference-call summary), light post-investment monitoring. Not a fund, not co-investment — pure advisory.

**ACV:** €60-€120K retainer; €150K premium for families doing 8+ deals/yr.

**Year-1:** 5 × €90K = €450K. Stretch 8 × €100K = €800K. Named list of ~30 is real; Dimitri reaches ~half via family + Greek-CEO-club ties.

**AI stack:** A1 ingests deal-flow from boutique IBs, PE secondaries, Greek/Cypriot DealRoom + pre-filters per family mandate. A2 builds first-draft memo (industry, competitive map, financials extraction, comparables). A3 synthesises reference-call notes. A4 maintains deal-monitoring tracker. Human: 6-10 reference calls per memo (Dimitri), final judgment + recommend/decline, committee presentation. AI 80%.

**Why Big 4 / boutique can't match cost:** Big 4 transaction advisory bills €350K+ per memo. Boutique IBs sell sell-side mandates. Actual competitor is the family's own analyst — one overloaded generalist. "Outsourced deal desk at €90K/yr" undercuts hiring a second analyst (€80-€120K all-in) AND delivers more breadth via AI.

**Why Dimitri-runnable:** Work is "senior judgment + curated information" — what AI amplifies for one experienced partner. 30-50 memos/year, AI-drafted, Dimitri-finished. No deal execution. No ops.

**T-Life edge:** Most family-network-load-bearing of the three. Greek family offices buy from people their cousins trust. Dimitri — fourth-gen, runs the family's own capital — is the exact peer signal. SaaS or Big 4 cannot replicate.

**Phase 0 (≤€50K, ≤16 wk):** Hypothesis: 2 family offices sign annual retainer ≥€50K each by Wk 16. Wk 1-4 build stack + 2 demo memos on real recent public Greek deals; Wk 5-10 meet 12-15 family offices via warm intros + pitch retainer; Wk 11-16 close 2. Cost: agents €15K + data (Pitchbook, Mergermarket, CapIQ) €18K + travel €5K + ops €7K = €45K. Kill: ≤1 retainer.

**p(red-team):** 0.55. Hits: (a) families may keep deal work in-house for confidentiality — mitigated by scoping confidential deals separately; (b) shape resembles a junior PE associate; may not scale past 8 clients without quality drop — hence honest cap €700-€800K; (c) Big 4 may launch "AI-augmented family-office desk" — possible but trust deficit is the moat.

**Founder-fit:** Strongest of the three. Dimitri already does this informally on T-Life's own deals; productizing for peers is low-friction. Enjoyment 8.5/10. Real risk: getting pulled into co-investment, turning advisory into deal-by-deal partnership — violates the services frame. Discipline: no carry, no co-invest. Cash retainers only.

## Why the other six listed sectors did not make this shortlist

- **EU pharma-policy advisory** — Excluded by "outside pharma"; re-tethers Dimitri to the identity he is diversifying from.
- **Boutique M&A sub-€20M EV** — Fails AI-80%+. Value capture on closings is human work.
- **AI-native specialty law** — Greek Bar unauthorized-practice rules make the retained attorney the bottleneck. Economics flip to attorney's solo practice; Dimitri's role becomes decorative.
- **AI-native CSR / ESG reporting** — Highest Big-4-productization risk. Deloitte Zora, EY ESG-Manager, KPMG Climate IQ attack this in 2025-26. Head-on collision the brief avoids.
- **Specialty patent / trademark filing** — Greek tech-IP volume too thin for €300K-€1.5M; natural buyer already with Drakopoulos/Patrinos/Vayanos.
- **Government affairs / lobbying** — Fails AI-80%+. Lobbying is relationship + calendar; AI can't replace partner meetings.
- *Commodities research for shipping/steel/energy considered as a 4th; borderline excluded — buyer overlap with #2 fragments attention; WTP unclear vs Trafigura/Glencore in-house desks. Revisit as #2 fallback.*
