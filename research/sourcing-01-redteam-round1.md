## Pattern observations (same as prior 9/9 cohort)

**The "Big 4 won't cannibalize" assumption is dead in 2025-2026.** It died in StateAidOps (Big 4 already bundling AI free with audit) AND in DG-Studies (Deloitte Zora $840M run-rate; EY.ai shipping). The 2026 reality is that Big 4 are cannibalizing their billable pyramids ahead of schedule — driven by client cost pressure post-RRF + the AI labour-pricing reset.

**The "regulated buyer can't be reached by SaaS at this price band" assumption is dead.** EpsilonNet ships PYLON Hybrid Agentic AI ERP at €X/month into Greek accountants; OneTrust ships DORA tooling at €1.5K/yr; Kickster has Piraeus office with 500+ vessels. Greek mid-market is not under-served in mid-2026.

**The "sovereign won't build this" assumption requires per-cluster validation.** It held in Aktis (Ministry of Shipping has no remit for commercial brokerage) but died in StateAidOps (ReGiS + OPS-TA are state-built) and Accountant-OS (AADE 2026 mandate ships free pre-filled VAT).

**The "load-bearing buyer segmentation" assumption needs primary verification, not desk research.** Aktis' "sub-30-vessel Piraeus owners" segment doesn't exist — those owners cluster at 90+ vessels through related entities. Sourcing agents working from public lists are vulnerable to this.

## What this means for the studio thesis

The 9-of-9 prior catalog kill + 4-of-5 sourcing-batch-01 kill rate (cumulative: 13/14 across two cohorts) is no longer noise. It is a structural finding:

> **In mid-2026, the obvious "gap in existing market" thesis is mostly foreclosed.** AI-native incumbents have shipped or are shipping faster than the sourcing methodology can identify gaps. Big 4 are cannibalizing their billables faster than the conventional "won't ship the cannibalizing product" assumption predicts. Sovereigns are building recipient-side tools, not just granting-side. M&A consolidation in 2024-2026 (Localyze→Boundless, Kpler/Spire, EpsilonNet roll-up, Entersoft+SoftOne, Cegid+Shine) has closed multiple wedges within the year the original sourcing was done.

**The right honest conclusion:** sourcing for "AI-native venture studio outside pharma" against the *generic* infrastructure-to-incumbents filter produces ~7-13% hit rate. That's not enough to build a portfolio at scale.

## Three honest paths forward

### Path A — Take DG-Studies-revise seriously

The DG-Studies alt-thesis (sell AI tooling TO the FWC primes — Deloitte, EY, Ramboll, ICF, Ecorys, Technopolis — as their EU-FWC-template-aware AI platform) is the ONLY survivor of this cycle.

It fits the morning's "infrastructure-to-incumbents" filter exactly (the buyers are the consultancies, not the EC). It dodges the FWC prime-entry barrier by selling to existing primes. Big 4 cannibalization is a tailwind, not a threat. The buyer set is small and named.

Plausible Phase 0: 16-week probe targeting 3-5 named primes for paid pilot, ≤€50K cost. If it converts, T-Life ships a v2 spec.

**Risk:** Big 4 build internally (Deloitte Zora is already in-house). Reply: the EU-FWC layer is small enough that none of the listed primes will fund its own dedicated tool stack; the consulting firms' AI investments are private-sector first, EU-FWC fifth or sixth priority.

### Path B — Change the sourcing methodology

The current sourcing brief identifies "gaps" in named markets. That methodology produces high kill rates because the gaps mostly aren't gaps.

A different sourcing brief would START from what only T-Life can do:
- **Acquisition-shaped bets** (MedAdherence-RWE pattern): use the family balance sheet to buy a tail vendor at €2-5M, gain a market position SaaS competitors can't reach because they don't have the capital structure
- **Family-network-edge bets:** start from the named senior-relationship list (Dimitri's father's generation Greek industrial / family-firm contacts) and source bets where those relationships are the moat
- **Dimitri-runnable bets:** AI-native services firms small enough Dimitri can operate them personally with 2-3 AI engineers — different cost structure than recruited-CEO ventures

This would produce a smaller, weirder shortlist but with higher hit rate.

### Path C — Accept the studio scope needs to evolve

Mid-2026 is structurally different from 2022. AI-native venture studios building NEW businesses outside the established AI-native incumbents' reach is harder than the 2022-2024 playbook suggests.

Honest paths inside Path C:
1. **The first studio bet might be pharma-adjacent after all** (despite the "outside pharma" preference) — because that's where the family's unique edge is genuinely uncontestable
2. **The first studio bet might be an acquisition not a build** — use the €160K reserve to find a sub-€5M EBITDA target during the consolidation window
3. **The studio mandate broadens to include holding-company-style bets** — buy and operate, not build from zero. Dimitri runs the studio as a private-equity-with-AI motion, not a venture-studio-with-AI motion
