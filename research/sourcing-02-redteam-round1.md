## Pattern observations (cumulative across 3 rounds)

### What killed candidates this round

1. **Stale target lists.** SpecialtyBroker died because Matrix was bought 6 years ago. AE-RollUp died because Salfo has internal succession (not what the spec claimed). Even agents with explicit "use 2024-2026 sources" briefs can miss recent M&A or current ownership. **Implication: every candidate needs a primary-source target-list verification BEFORE Phase 0.**
2. **Regulatory timeline shifts.** AI Act Digital AI Omnibus moved deadlines 12-18 months out, deleting the urgency that powered the AI Act+DORA Conformity thesis Year-1 math. **Implication: theses anchored on near-term regulatory cliffs are uniquely fragile because regulators move them on political timelines outside our control.**
3. **Buyer persona inflation.** Family-Office Memos overstated the addressable family-office count by ~10× — Latsis/Niarchos/Demetra aren't in the spec's persona, they have in-house teams or use Big 4. **Implication: "named buyer list" must be verified at the persona level (do they actually buy this category of service?), not just the entity level (does the entity exist?).**

### What batch-02 v2 brief got right vs batch-01

| Pattern | Batch 01 result | Batch 02 result |
|---|---|---|
| Top 5 round-1 hit rate | 1 of 5 viable (DG-Studies REVISE only) | 3 of 5 viable (Family-Office, AE-RollUp, Tender-Writing all REVISE) |
| Average reframe size | Major (DG-Studies → sell to FWC primes) | Minor (smaller ambition, tighter scope) |
| Failures from "incumbent ships in 12 months" | 3 of 4 kills | 0 of 2 kills |
| Failures from "target list is stale or wrong-persona" | 0 of 4 kills | 2 of 2 kills |
| Failures from "regulatory tailwind moved" | 0 of 4 kills | 1 of 2 kills |

The v2 brief structurally dodged "incumbent ships in 12 months" (the main batch-01 killer) — because family-network + acquisition + Dimitri-runnable variants don't compete with incumbents on product features. **They die instead on factual errors (stale lists, persona inflation) or external timing (regulatory shifts).**

This is meaningful: factual errors are fixable with primary-source diligence; external timing is sometimes survivable with re-scoping. Both are softer kill modes than "Veeva/Toast/Lloyd's already shipped it."

### The REVISE candidates have a clear pattern

All 3 REVISE alt-theses are **smaller-ambition reframes** of the original. None propose a structurally-different business — they propose the same business at:
- Lower revenue ceiling (€500-700K vs original €800K-1.4M)
- Tighter client count (2-3 anchors vs 8)
- Different sequencing (sell-then-buy vs buy-then-rebuild)
- Different revenue model (fixed retainer vs hybrid success fee)

The original specs were aspirational; the reframes are realistic. **This is the right direction for Dimitri-runnable bets** — €500-700K cash to one operator is genuinely good money, even if it's not venture-shaped.
