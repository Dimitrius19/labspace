# Govtech / Public-Sector AI Automation — Candidate Theses (T-Life Capital)

Govtech is the highest-kill cluster: sovereigns build their own platforms (gov.gr, FranceConnect, IO, Verwaltungsportal, Arachne); where they don't, SAP/Oracle/Microsoft public-sector SORs absorb the AI layer. The surviving lane is **arm's-length, procurement-law-mandated services work that cannot be in-housed** — the consulting/CRO bill paid to Deloitte/EY/Accenture/KPMG/PwC — plus regulated private intermediaries whose buyers are the state.

Three clear pre-screen, one borderline. Killed candidates listed at end.

---

## 1. DG-Studies — AI-native services-CRO for EU Commission impact assessments, ex-post evaluations, and compliance studies

**Buyer set (named procurement routes):** European Commission Framework Contracts under SC ("studies and consultancy") lots — specifically DG SANTE FWC SANTE/2023/EHF, DG GROW FWC GROW/2023/SME-IMPACT, DG EMPL FWC VC/2022/0123, DG CONNECT FWC SMART 2023/0067, JRC and Eurofound retainers. Secondary: ECHA, EFSA, EMA scientific-services frameworks. Mode of entry: subcontractor on existing Deloitte/EY/Ramboll/ICF lots first (FWC cascade allows it), then prime on lot renewals 2027–2028.

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** Deloitte/EY bill €1,400–2,000/hr senior, €600–900/hr junior. A typical ex-post evaluation is €0.8–2.5M: stakeholder mapping, literature review, 27-MS transposition comparison, survey synthesis, CBA — 70–80% is structured RAG/summarization/tabulation. AI does that layer at ~€20/hr equivalent; the 20% senior judgement/political-phrasing remains human. Margin gap 8–12×.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** Deloitte/EY public-sector P&Ls are bodies × utilization; cannibalizing their own pyramid needs board approval. They will "AI-enable consultants" while preserving the bill. Internal tools (Deloitte Zora, EY.ai) target private sector first. FWC scoring rewards methodological novelty and reference list — both attackable.

**Why this sovereign cannot/will not build it free:** EU Financial Regulation Art. 160 + procurement law require **external, independent** evaluators for ex-post and impact assessment. JRC and Eurofound are capacity-capped and cannot scale to ~€600M/yr DG studies spend. Sovereign-build is legally foreclosed.

**Structural defensibility:** (a) FWC cascade membership = 4-year contractual lock; (b) corpus of MS-transposition mappings, stakeholder graph, prior-evaluation embeddings compounds per DG; (c) reference list qualifies you for next lot; (d) EU security clearances (CONFIDENTIEL UE).

**T-Life asset edge:** Tryfon family Brussels reach via EFPIA-adjacent pharma policy contacts is real for DG SANTE entry. Greek presence helps on Greek-language MS-transposition work. Family balance-sheet covers the 90–180-day EU payment cycle.

**Phase 0 kill test (≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):** Subcontract on one named ongoing FWC task (DG SANTE or DG GROW) via an existing prime — deliver one lit-review + transposition-mapping package at 1/8 labor budget, accepted in writing by DG desk officer. Binary: signed acceptance, or kill.

**p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.45. Risks: (a) big-four cannibalize faster than expected by 2027; (b) FWC primes refuse to subcontract a competitor-in-waiting; (c) 2026 EU procurement reform changes FWC structure.

## 2. JudgeStack — AI litigation-analytics and case-prep platform for private law firms and corporates pleading before EU/national courts

**Buyer set (named procurement routes):** NOT the courts (they build e-CODEX / e-Justice themselves). Buyers are **private**: AmLaw100 Brussels offices, magic-circle EU competition/state-aid practices, in-house legal at Fortune 500 EU-regulated entities (energy, pharma, tech under DMA/DSA, banking under CRR/CRD). Procurement is private B2B at €150K–800K ACV per firm, multi-seat. Adjacent regulated buyer: national bar associations licensing the tool to members (Greek DSA, Ordre des Avocats Paris, CCBE).

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** Magic-circle associates at €450–700/hr spend 60% of pre-hearing time on case-law retrieval (CJEU + national supreme + AG opinions), chronology tables, adversary-brief synthesis — RAG-shaped. Replace 40% of associate hours at 1/20 cost; €600/seat/month × thousands of seats.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** Thomson Reuters, LexisNexis+ AI, vLex Vincent ship — but on US/UK common-law data models. CJEU specificity (Art. 263 admissibility, AG opinions, 24-language authentic texts, preliminary-ruling chains) is poorly modeled. Harvey is US-firm-centric. The CJEU-native + multilingual + AG-chain angle is open.

**Why this sovereign cannot/will not build it free:** CJEU and national courts ship CURIA, e-Justice, EUR-Lex — all public-search tools. They will not build litigation strategy / adversary-prediction (judicial neutrality). Bar associations lack capability and revenue model. Structurally private-sector.

**Structural defensibility:** (a) Proprietary structured DB of CJEU procedural metadata (admissibility outcomes, AG-Court divergence rates per chamber/topic); (b) network effect: anonymized adversary briefs improve corpus; (c) bar-association exclusive licensing.

**T-Life asset edge:** Limited. Family pharma counsel network (Bird & Bird, Sidley, Arnold & Porter on EMA matters) gives 3–5 design-partner intros. Otherwise competes on product.

**Phase 0 kill test (≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):** 3 paid design-partner contracts at ≥€25K each from named EU competition/state-aid practices (Cleary, Linklaters Brussels, Van Bael & Bellis, Hogan Lovells Brussels). Binary: 3 signed POs, or kill.

**p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.35. Risks: Harvey/vLex move into CJEU faster; magic-circle firms build in-house; arguably "private legaltech" not govtech — may belong in another cluster.

## 3. StateAidOps — AI compliance, notification, and reporting automation for recipients of EU state aid, RRF, and cohesion funds

**Buyer set:** Recipients, not granting authorities (Arachne owns that side). (a) **Greek RRF beneficiaries** — primes on €30.5B plan (TERNA, Mytilineos, PPC, GEK TERNA), each running €50–500M projects with quarterly DNSH/milestone reporting; (b) **Italian PNRR beneficiaries** — €191B, named primes Webuild, Enel, Snam; (c) **EU Innovation Fund and Horizon Europe consortium coordinators**. B2B sale, buyer-of-record is project PMO.

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** PwC/EY/KPMG charge €300K–1.5M per large RRF/PNRR project for quarterly reporting, DNSH memos, audit-trail prep, Commission liaison. 80% structured: pulling invoices, mapping to budget lines, generating DNSH narratives, Annex VI templates. AI at 5% cost; we charge €60–150K/yr, 5–10× margin.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** Big-four won't cannibalize. SAP/Oracle ERPs handle financials, not compliance narrative. Italian/Greek niche (Lattanzio, Planet) are consultancies with no product motion.

**Why this sovereign cannot/will not build it free:** Arachne is **anti-fraud for granting authorities** — conflict-of-interest and double-funding detection. It does **not** help recipients prepare submissions. Commission/MS will not provide recipient-side compliance tools (creates state liability for compliance failures). Audit requires recipient-side independent capability — exactly the gap.

**Structural defensibility:** (a) Once 18 months of project data is in, switching mid-project is unthinkable; (b) programme-period contracts run to 2026/2029 — multi-year by design; (c) Commission template changes 2x/yr create re-learning moat; (d) certification angle via national audit authorities.

**T-Life asset edge:** Family business network has direct relationships with named RRF primes (pharma family knows TERNA, Mytilineos, PPC at owner level — Greek industrial elite is small). Family balance sheet covers slow corporate procurement.

**Phase 0 kill test (≤€50K, ≤16 weeks):** 2 paid pilots at €25K each with named Greek RRF beneficiaries (TERNA, Mytilineos, PPC, GEK TERNA, or comparable) for one reporting cycle. Binary: signed pilot agreements with go-live dates, or kill.

**p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.50. Strongest of the four. Risks: (a) RRF ends 2026, must pivot to next MFF; (b) big-four bundle AI free with existing audit.

## 4. (Borderline) NotaryStack — AI document-prep, due-diligence and filing automation for Greek/Italian/French notaries, land registries, and conveyancing

**Buyer set:** Greek Notarial Associations (Athens, Thessaloniki — ~3,500 notaries), Consiglio Nazionale del Notariato (Italy, ~5,000 notaries), Conseil Supérieur du Notariat (France, ~17,000 notaries). Adjacent: Hellenic Cadastre (Ktimatologio), Agenzia delle Entrate land registry.

**Why borderline:** Notaries are regulated private monopolies with state-backed fee schedules — they have the budget and the bottleneck (each closing is 40–80 hours of paralegal work). BUT: each association may try to build a shared platform free for members (Greek Notarial Association tendered something in 2024). And the Italian Notartel cooperative already runs a shared digital backbone. Risk of sovereign-equivalent free build is medium-high.

**Phase 0 kill test:** 5 paid pilots at €10K each across two countries within 16 weeks. **p(survives): 0.25.** Park, revisit if NotaryStack-style consolidation evidence emerges in 2026.
