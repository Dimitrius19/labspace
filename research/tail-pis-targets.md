# Greek Pharmacy IT (PIS) — Tail-Vendor Acquisition Target Shortlist
**Prepared for:** T-Life Capital — MedAdherence-RWE Phase 0 / Phase 1
**Deal box:** €2-5M EV, sidestep Epsilon NET / Epsilon CSA stack
**Date:** May 2026

## Market context (confirmed)
- ~10,400 retail pharmacies in Greece.
- **Epsilon CSA (Epsilon NET Group)** is the dominant consolidator with **>50% share / 5,200+ pharmacies** on the Farmakon / PYLON Farmakon Hybrid stack. Acquired CSA in 2022; LAVINET Jan 2024 (€184K, ~150 pharmacy chain-network seats); Galaxy Pharma via the €18.05M SingularLogic SPA. Confirmed.
- **ILYDA / Dioscourides** publicly claims 70% of pharmacies on at least one of its solutions and 1,250+ pharmacies on Dioscourides retail (the two claims overlap with CSA's — see notes).
- **Entersoft acquired SmartPharmaSys/SmartMediSys/SmartRetail from Smartware International** Nov 2022 for **€1.8M + earnout**, ~€1.1M revenue / €0.22M EBITDA. Now part of Entersoft's small-business cloud line.
- The remaining "tail" outside CSA + ILYDA + Entersoft is fragmented and mostly founder-owned, sub-€2M revenue regional shops.

## Acquirability narrative per live candidate

**1. ILYDA / Dioscourides (MEDIUM, MEDIUM confidence).** ILYDA is the only credible at-scale non-Epsilon pharmacy player and the only one with audited public financials (ATHEX-listed). Founder remains President with high insider concentration, which both signals continuity and gates any deal. The 65/35 Dioscourides AE sub-structure is the most interesting angle: the 35% minority could be bought for a fraction of the listco market cap, giving T-Life a board seat and RWE data-rights conversation without a control premium. Full take-private exceeds the €2-5M box; minority/JV structure is the only realistic path.

**2. Europharmacy / euromedica (HIGH acquirability, MEDIUM confidence).** Thessaloniki-based IKE producing the euromedica TWO pharmacy suite, awarded "Platinum" by Greek pharmacists three consecutive years. No PE backer, no acquisition history, founder-led, single-member legal form — textbook tail-vendor profile for a €2-5M tuck-in. Seat count is the unknown; PFS-poll Platinum status implies a meaningful (but not dominant) installed base. Diligence priorities: actual seat count, IDIKA certification status, source-code/IP ownership versus subcontractors, founder succession intent.

**3. In-house Solutions / E-Care (HIGH acquirability, MEDIUM confidence).** Greek generalist IT house (inhouse.gr) with a pharmacy vertical — E-Care, marketed with eRx, e-signature, GDPR, and inventory-velocity modules. Generalist owners often divest single verticals once a strategic buyer surfaces. Likely sub-€2M revenue in the pharmacy line; price could land at the low end of the box. Diligence priorities: pharmacy seat count, whether E-Care is sold as a stand-alone or only with the broader inhouse stack, and customer concentration.

**4. Smartware International residual (LOW).** Pharmacy IP gone to Entersoft. Only worth a conversation if a medical-practice or RWE-adjacent residual module remains and Entersoft's non-compete has expired.

**5. CloudOn / PharmacyOne (LOW-MEDIUM).** Buying CloudOn is buying an implementation business, not the IP (Soft1 belongs to SoftOne SA, ATHEX-listed). The Pharmacy295 reference is valuable for RWE data access into Greece's largest e-pharmacy. Treat as a partnership / data-licensing target, not an acquisition.

## Recommended Phase 0 actions
1. **Europharmacy** and **In-house Solutions** are the two clean €2-5M box targets — initiate outreach.
2. **Dioscourides AE 35% minority** is the only way to get scale-meaningful seat coverage within budget; structure as a strategic minority + RWE data-rights agreement with ILYDA.
3. Confirm seat counts via IDIKA — the prescription-API connection registry is the definitive source of certified vendor identities. Filing an FOI / direct request to IDIKA is the fastest disambiguation.
