## 1. Aktis — Shipping FuelEU Maritime + EU-ETS broker-of-record for second-tier Piraeus owners
**Operator-of-record for emissions allowances, FuelEU pooling, and bunker-swap arbitrage for sub-fleet (<30 vessel) Greek owners.**

**Buyer set (named):**
- Dorian LPG (~24 VLGCs) — est. €400–700K/yr
- Pantheon Tankers (Thenamaris affiliate, ~15) — €300K
- Eletson Holdings (~25 tankers, restructured 2024) — €350K
- Avin International (Vardinoyannis, ~13) — €250K
- Centrofin (Procopiou, ~25) — €400K

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** Voyage CO2 attribution, FuelEU well-to-wake intensity, and pooling counterparty matching are today done by 3-5 person in-house emissions desks or Big 4 at €1–2k/vessel/month. One AI agent per fleet consumes noon reports, AIS, BDNs and produces verified MRV/FuelEU submissions, EUA tickets, pool offers. Unit cost falls from ~€20K/vessel/yr to ~€2K.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** DNV Emissions Connect/Veracity, ZeroNorth ship verifier + dashboards, not regulated brokerage. ShipFORCE, Vessel.Report are noon-report tools. None hold an EU-ETS trading licence or run a FuelEU pool book. Becoming counterparty (not SaaS) requires MiFID II 2(1)(j) ancillary-activity analysis + EUA registry access — 12+ months of risk setup incumbents avoid because it dilutes SaaS margin.

**Why no sovereign supplies free:** THETIS-MRV is verification only; Ministry of Shipping has no remit; activity is by definition commercial brokerage.

**Structural defensibility:** Relationships (sub-30-vessel Piraeus is relationship-bought) + capital (we float the EUA inventory) + regulatory (EUA broker + verifier partnership).

**T-Life asset edge:** High. 4-gen Greek industrial credibility unlocks meetings US/UK SaaS vendors cannot get; family balance sheet can carry €5–15M EUA float pure SaaS can't.

**Phase 0 kill test:**
- Hypothesis: 3+ named sub-30-vessel Piraeus owners will sign a 12-month exclusive emissions-ops + brokerage mandate at >€300K ACV after a single 30-day pilot on 2 vessels.
- Experiment: Hire 1 ex-Arcadia/Dorian emissions ops person; build the voyage-attribution agent on top of Veracity API; pitch the 12-name target list cold + via 2 family-referred intros.
- KillSignal: <2 signed mandates after 16 weeks.
- ValidateSignal: ≥3 signed mandates with ≥€900K combined ACV; verified pricing power vs. internal cost.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.55

## 2. Astynomia — Greek Golden Visa + EU long-stay back-office factory
**Vertically integrated "AI paralegal + licensed Greek lawyer of record" that takes Golden Visa / Digital Nomad / Type-D applications from intake to permit at fixed price, undercutting Greek lawyers 60%.**

**Buyer set (named):**
- Henley & Partners (Athens, ~600 GV files/yr) — €1.2M
- Astons (Limassol/Athens, ~400) — €700K
- Get Golden Visa (Lisbon, ~300) — €500K
- La Vida Golden Visas (UK, ~250) — €400K
- Direct B2C Mandarin/Arabic/Hebrew SEO (~200) — €600K
Addressable: ~7K main-investor apps/yr (6,978 in 2025 per Migration Ministry).

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** A Greek GV file requires ~25 paralegal hrs (translations, apostilles, AFM, tax-rep, notary, e-permit). Greek firms charge €5–8K at ~50% margin. AI agent does intake, completeness check, AML source-of-funds pre-screen, deed draft, e-permit submission, polling. Lawyer of record signs off in <1 hr. Unit cost €3K → €400; we charge €1,800.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** Thomson Reuters/Lexis don't touch Greek migration; Bernitsas/KG/Sarantitis won't cannibalise €6K fees; international RBI agencies (Henley etc.) need a Greek-bar counterparty and don't want to be it. No SOR ships this.

**Why no sovereign supplies free:** Migration Ministry portal is the *destination*, not the service; sovereign can't represent investors before itself.

**Structural defensibility:** Regulatory (Greek bar admission + attorney-of-record cannot be unbundled) + data (AML rejection-pattern library after ~500 files) + relationships (exclusive feeder contracts with 2-3 RBI agencies).

**T-Life asset edge:** Medium. Family credibility helps recruit attorney-of-record at sub-market comp; unlocks Athens/Thessaloniki notary network.

**Phase 0 kill test:**
- Hypothesis: 2 international RBI agencies will sign exclusive Greek-leg subcontract at €1,800/file fixed price for ≥150 files combined annual commitment within 16 weeks.
- Experiment: Recruit lawyer-of-record; ship intake + AML pre-screen + e-permit submission agent on 10 dummy files; pitch 8 named agencies.
- KillSignal: 0 signed agency contracts OR client-acquisition cost > €600/file via direct channel.
- ValidateSignal: ≥150 files/yr committed AND end-to-end unit cost <€500.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.45

**Buyer set (named):**
- Optima Bank (Athens) — €250K
- Pancreta Bank (Crete) — €180K
- Attica Bank / Pancreta merged — €300K
- Euronet Greek e-money entity — €120K
- Hellenic Post Bank — €150K

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** DORA Art. 28 requires a ~70-field register-of-information per ICT contract; Art. 19 demands 4hr/24hr/1mo incident notifications. **Structural defensibility:** Regulatory (Greek-incorporated service contracting model, mirroring post-GDPR DPO market) + relationships (named Greek mid-tier bank CIOs, ~30 people fully coverable) + data (Bank of Greece response-pattern library after first 5 filings).

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.5

## 4. Theta — EU AI Act + DPIA Article-27 fundamental-rights impact factory for Greek public-sector AI buyers
**Mandatory FRIA/DPIA/Article-27 documentation as-a-service for the ~80 Greek public bodies and concession-holders procuring AI systems under the AI Act high-risk regime.**

**Buyer set (named):**
- IDIKA (e-health AI triage / e-prescription) — €200K
- ELGA (agri-insurance AI claims) — €150K
- AADE (tax-fraud AI models) — €300K
- Attiko Metro / OSE (predictive maintenance) — €180K
- Athens Intl Airport (biometrics + ops AI) — €120K
- ~12 municipalities deploying AI under Recovery Plan

**Why AI-native wins on unit cost:** A FRIA + DPIA + AI Act Annex IV pack costs €60–120K from Big 4 (4–8 wks). Work is heavily templated: risk taxonomy, data-governance assertions, post-market monitoring, residual-risk register. AI agent ingests model card + procurement spec + DPA + dataset description, drafts the 80-page pack against ISO/IEC 42001 + Annex IV; senior reviews in 4 hrs. Cost €60K → €4K; we charge €15–25K.

**Why incumbents can't ship this in 12 months:** PwC/Deloitte are the incumbents; can't productise below billable rate. OneTrust ships GRC tooling — doesn't sign deliverables. No SOR accepts liability for Greek-language regulatory submissions. EU AI Office is guidance-only.

**Why no sovereign supplies free:** HDPA supervises, doesn't author DPIAs/FRIAs; National AI Office is guidance only; conflict-of-interest bar is explicit in AI Act Art. 27.

**Structural defensibility:** Regulatory (independent-assessor roster of ~6 credentialled humans = moat) + data (Greek public-procurement AI corpus; each Annex IV file cheapens the next) + relationships (family network into 4–5 named buyers).

**T-Life asset edge:** Medium-high. Greek family network into public procurement is exactly where sovereign-grade trust matters; foreign vendors lose structurally.

**Phase 0 kill test:**
- Hypothesis: 2 named Greek public/concession buyers will sign a paid FRIA/Annex-IV deliverable at ≥€20K within 16 weeks following 1 free sample pack delivered to a friendly entity.
- Experiment: Recruit 1 ex-HDPA / ex-Bank of Greece AI-risk lead; ship the Annex-IV draft agent on a real friendly-test dataset; pitch 8 named procurement leads.
- KillSignal: 0 signed paid contracts after 16 weeks OR average price <€8K (commoditised).
- ValidateSignal: ≥2 contracts ≥€20K each, pipeline ≥€1M, no Big-4 underbidding observed.

**Honest p(survives 2-round red-team):** 0.4
