# Greek govtech incumbents map

Who already sells software & services into the Greek public sector. The Red Team agent uses this to flag incumbent overlap.

## Tier-1 system integrators (deep ministry penetration)

- **Intracom Telecom / Intrasoft International** — historically the largest govtech SI in Greece. Built early tax systems, Διαύγεια, ΑΑΔΕ TAXIS/TAXISnet. Now part of Netcompany (Danish) after the Intrasoft acquisition. Will bid on every framework tender.
- **OTE/Cosmote** — telecoms and infrastructure plays; runs major outsourced contracts for ministries via Cosmote e-Value. Strong on call centres, gov.gr telephony rails.
- **Singular Logic** — ERP + electoral systems (notorious — 2019 election results glitch). Strong relationships with Interior ministry.
- **Space Hellas** — cybersecurity, network infra for Defence and Citizen Protection.
- **Byte Computer** — long-tail systems integration, often subcontractor on big deals.
- **Performance Technologies** — managed services, healthcare and tax.
- **Quality & Reliability (Q&R)** — ESPA/RRF disbursement plumbing — heavily entrenched.
- **Uni Systems** — Quest group; large gov data centre and Cassandra/Lefkippos work.
- **Cyberis / Encode** — cybersecurity boutiques on defence/police accounts.

## Newer / digital-native players

- **Beat (formerly Taxibeat)** — owned by Free Now; not govtech but the demo of "Greek-language consumer product that worked." Often cited as the gov.gr design inspiration benchmark.
- **Workable** — Greek-origin SaaS; not in govtech but a credibility reference for the founder pitch.
- **InstaShop / Wolt** — same — operational scale references.
- **Atypon / John Wiley** — publishing platforms; Atypon brain trust is the most underrated Greek deep-tech engineering bench. Hireable.

## Govtech-specific newer entrants

- **GRNet (ΕΔΥΤΕ)** — public-sector tech body; runs the educational network and provides cloud to ministries. Sometimes builds, sometimes buys. Friendly to startups via PoC routes.
- **Code4Greece / Civic-tech NGOs** — small; mostly advocacy. Not commercial competitors.
- **AthTech** — Athens-based tech consultancy with civic contracts.
- **Profile Software** — fintech-focused but bids on AADE adjacent work.

## International players active in Greek public tenders

- **Accenture, Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY** — all "consulting plus implementation." They get strategy work; rarely ship custom agentic systems.
- **Microsoft Greece, AWS Greece** — landed-zone deals; the new Microsoft Azure region in Athens is the substrate, not the competitor.
- **Palantir** — has been circling Greek defence / migration but no major announced wins as of 2026.
- **Salesforce, ServiceNow** — case management systems in some ministries; surface area for agents on top of these platforms.

## Where incumbents DO NOT play well

This is the blue-ocean overlay:

- **Greek-language LLM-native workflows.** All the SIs above are integrators, not model labs. None have fine-tuned models on Greek admin language.
- **End-to-end agentic loops** (vs. dashboard / form-builder products).
- **Inter-ministerial workflows** that cross system boundaries (gov.gr handles citizen-side; back-office crossings are everyone's-and-no-one's).
- **Field-mobile agents** — civil protection, agriculture inspections, port operations. Mostly paper today.
- **EU-funds compliance & audit-trail generation** — Q&R does disbursement but not audit-trail automation.
- **Judicial drafting / jurisprudence search in Greek.** Lexis/Westlaw don't ship Greek; Nomos/ΝΟΜΟΣ is a database, not a copilot.

## How to read this

If your idea overlaps with a Tier-1 SI's known surface area (e.g. "TAXISnet replacement"), you're going to lose a tender. Don't pitch the replacement — pitch the **agent that sits on top of it** and makes the existing system 10× more productive. That's a procurement carve-out story (innovation pilot, ESPA-funded PoC), not a framework-tender story.
