# Business Plan

## Company thesis
**Tranchewire** is an agentic-AI company that turns Greece's six-monthly EU-funds reporting from a quarterly fire-drill into a continuous, audit-grade reporting flow. The wedge is the RRF payment-request submission — one document, twice a year, worth a €3-4bn tranche each time — but the **company is built for what comes after 31 Aug 2026**, when the RRF instrument closes. The same agent loop retargets onto the ESPA 2021-2027 indicator framework (which runs to ~2029 under the n+3 rule) and is positioned to inherit the post-2027 cohesion-funds successor instrument under the 2028-2034 MFF, which the Commission has signalled will preserve the milestone-and-target architecture. The qualification of this idea moved from PASS to CONDITIONAL (composite 3.78) precisely on instrument-expiry risk; this memo addresses the bridge head-on.

Concretely: Tranchewire sits on top of OPS, Διαύγεια, ministry portals and the evidence repository; it reads the CID and Operational Arrangement; it drafts the Commission semi-annual submission with a traceable citation chain that meets the ECA Special Report 14/2026 (traceability and transparency) and 06/2026 (anti-fraud) bar. Get RRF right in the residual two cycles, and we are the only Greek-deployed agent with a battle-tested reporting loop just as the cohesion programming pivot lands.

## Market & buyer
Primary buyer for the RRF wedge: the **Special Secretariat for RRF** at the Ministry of National Economy & Finance. Secondary buyer, and primary buyer post-2026: the **GS for Public Investments**, who owns ESPA 2021-2027 absorption and will own the cohesion-funds successor. The Commission counterpart (DG ECFIN for RRF, DG REGIO/DG EMPL for cohesion) is the standard the work is graded against, never the buyer.

The buyer's pain is concrete and recurring. Every six months the Special Secretariat assembles an evidence pack covering each milestone with an upcoming deadline, writes the per-milestone fulfilment summary in EN+GR, and ships to DG ECFIN. Today this is email-chasing thirteen line ministries, hand-built spreadsheets, and four to six weeks of war-room. The political cost of a late or rejected tranche is paid in press cycles; Greece is the #2 RRP per-capita in the EU and the press tracks delays quarterly. Portugal's 8th payment request was positively assessed in Jan 2026 with 196 milestones still remaining — the proof point that this pain runs to the wire.

There is no competing Greek-deployed agent. Italy's ReGiS (mandatory for line ministries since 2022) and Portugal's Recuperar Portugal are tracking systems; neither drafts the Commission submission from primary evidence. That work remains manual on the member-state side everywhere. The ECA's 2026 traceability and fraud reports raise the audit bar — they help, not hurt, an audit-trail-by-design product.

## The 2026-08-31 bridge (the binding question)
The RRF instrument expires 31 Aug 2026. Final payment claims tail into late 2026; the audit window then runs to roughly 2030. Stated bluntly: by the time we sign a pilot in Q3-2026, the named RRF surface area is one residual payment cycle plus a multi-year audit tail. That alone is not a venture-grade reference. The bridge is therefore the thesis.

**Bridge anchor 1 — ESPA 2021-2027 indicator framework.** ESPA runs to ~2029 under the n+3 closure rule. CPR Art. 16/22 defines a milestone-and-target indicator structure (output and result indicators per priority) that is structurally analogous to the RRF milestone schema: deadlines, evidence types, fulfilment summaries, performance-framework reviews. The agent loop — ingest indicator register, hunt evidence across OPS/Διαύγεια/KIMDIS, score, draft — retargets without rewriting. The buyer shifts from Special Secretary RRF to GS Public Investments. The data shape differs in roughly 25-30% of fields (regional OP indicators, performance-framework rather than payment-request cadence) — non-trivial but a refactor, not a rebuild.

**Bridge anchor 2 — post-2027 cohesion successor.** The Commission has telegraphed that the 2028-2034 MFF will keep performance-based disbursement tied to milestones-and-targets (the RRF method has become institutional muscle). Whatever the successor instrument is named, it will require member-state-side draft-generation against an indicator register. We will be the only vendor with operational reference deployments and a Greek-language audit-trail moat when the programming dust settles in 2027-2028.

**Bridge anchor 3 — ECA-driven audit tail (2026-2030).** ECA SR 14/2026 (traceability) and SR 06/2026 (fraud) put pressure on member states to evidence every disbursed euro on demand. Even after the RRF instrument closes, the audit-trail synthesis surface persists for four+ years. Tranchewire's evidence taxonomy and citation manifest become an audit-pack generator with no rebuild.

The CONDITIONAL composite of 3.78 stands until the founder hunt closes; the bridge is what converts CONDITIONAL into PASS at re-qualification.

## Product (agentic loop)
Tranchewire is a closed loop with seven steps, all auditable, all instrument-agnostic by construction:

1. **Ingest** the active instrument register — RRP CID+OA today, ESPA 2021-2027 performance framework tomorrow, cohesion successor in 2028. Cluster the ~330 RRF milestones (or ~600 ESPA indicators) by responsible desk.
2. **Hunt evidence** for each milestone/indicator with an upcoming deadline across OPS, Διαύγεια, ministry portals, KIMDIS, ESIDIS where relevant, and the evidence-document repository.
3. **Score** each milestone/indicator red/amber/green with a citation chain back to source document and timestamp. Every claim is one click from a primary artefact and a SHA hash.
4. **Draft** the per-milestone fulfilment summary or per-indicator performance-framework narrative in EN+GR in the active template — Commission payment-request register today, ESPA Annual Implementation Report register tomorrow.
5. **Flag** at-risk items with a remediation memo to the responsible GS: what is missing, who owns it, how many days remain.
6. **Assemble** the consolidated dossier with an audit-grade evidence index meeting ECA SR 14/2026 traceability standards.
7. **Publish** a public-facing tracker to the transparency portal after each cycle.

Two non-negotiables: the loop is never autonomous — every output is reviewed by a Special-Secretariat or GS-Public-Investments analyst before the Commission sees it; and audit-trail-by-design is enforced at the schema level. Italy's ReGiS is mandatory tracking, no agentic drafting; we sit alongside it conceptually, not against it.

## Revenue model & pricing
Three contract tiers, all designed to survive instrument transitions:

- **RRF Pilot (Q3-2026, €45-60k, direct-award)**: two line ministries × one residual tranche cycle. Scoped under the €60k απευθείας ανάθεση threshold so it can be signed without an open call. Deliverable: agent-produced evidence pack + fulfilment summary for in-scope milestones; ≥80% acceptance into the submission with edits only; analyst time down ≥70%.
- **ESPA bridge framework (H1-2027, €350-500k/yr)**: open competition under standard public-procurement law, scoped to ESPA 2021-2027 indicator reporting + RRF audit-trail synthesis. Pre-market consultation with GS Public Investments shapes the spec. Multi-year through 2029.
- **Cohesion successor (2028+, €500k-1m/yr)**: positioned at programming-design phase to be the reference draft-generation layer for the 2028-2034 MFF performance instrument across Greece, with country-localised exports.

Beyond Greece: same product, country-localised, sold to Cyprus, Malta, Croatia, Bulgaria, Romania, Slovakia — small national plans with no in-house ReGiS-style stack. Conservative: three additional country deployments by 2029 at €200-400k each, on the cohesion bridge rather than RRF.

## Unit economics
At framework scale, gross margin ~75%. Cloud + LLM inference per reporting cycle ~€15k; the rest is human time — analyst-onboarding, prompt-engineering against each new template, white-glove review of the first two cycles in each instrument. Contract LTV per country across RRF tail + ESPA + cohesion successor: €3-5m through 2032. CAC is sales-team time; there are ~10 buyers in Europe for RRF, ~27 for cohesion-funds, all reachable by name.

## Capital plan & milestones
- **Pre-seed (now, €350k)**: founder-led, two engineers, six-month sprint to a Q3-2026 RRF pilot and a Q1-2027 ESPA bridge memo to GS Public Investments. Founder + 1 senior engineer + 1 part-time EU-law counsel.
- **Seed (H2-2027, €1.5m)**: team to eight, ESPA framework signed, RRF pilot replayed as audit-trail synthesis on the audit tail, first non-Greek country in commercial discussions.
- **Series A optionality (2028)**: post-2027 cohesion successor programming concludes; we sell into the new instrument at scale. Series A depends on whether 3+ country deployments and Greek ESPA framework are live by then.

Key non-financial milestones: signed RRF pilot Q3-2026; first Commission submission through the agent H1-2027; ESPA framework signature H2-2027; cohesion-successor reference architecture published 2028.

## Team & hires
Founding team needs three names: (i) an ex-Special-Secretariat-RRF-or-ESPA technocrat as CEO who has personally lived through a payment-request cycle AND an ESPA Annual Implementation Report cycle — the bridge profile (sub-5-name list in Athens, qualification flagged this as the binding constraint); (ii) a senior engineer from the Workable/Beat/Persado bench who can ship Greek-aware document pipelines; (iii) an EU-funds lawyer on retainer for OA and CPR Art. 16/22 edge cases. First two hires post-pilot: a second engineer and an analyst who has worked inside a line-ministry RRF coordinator desk or an OP Managing Authority.

The 30-day founder hunt closes the qualification. Without that signature, we do not approach the Special Secretariat.

## Risks (with bridge as the headline)
- **Instrument expiry (binding)** — RRF closes 31 Aug 2026; the named surface dies. Counter: the entire product is engineered as instrument-agnostic; ESPA 2021-2027 + cohesion successor are the durable substrates. The pilot wins us the operational reference; the bridge wins us the company.
- **Incumbent overlap** — Q&R and Netcompany/Intrasoft sit inside OPS; buyer may ask "why not absorb this into OPS for the residual cycles." Counter: we are evidence-extraction and draft-generation above OPS, never a replacement; OPS stays the system of record.
- **Founder-market fit (constraint)** — sub-5-name list of bridge-profile technocrats. Counter: 30-day hunt with two parallel warm paths; no pilot pitch until the seat is filled.
- **Regulatory bar** — ECA SR 14/2026 + 06/2026 raise the audit standard. Counter: audit-trail-by-design from day one, with OA + CPR-defined evidence taxonomy as the schema. This is a tailwind, not a risk.
- **Political turnover** — change of minister mid-pilot can stall payment. Counter: anchor at Special-Secretariat / GS Public Investments level, not minister; both seats survive reshuffles.
- **EU AI Act** — Annex III enforcement deferred to 2 Dec 2027 (Omnibus deal 7 May 2026), giving us a clean 18-month onramp before classification questions hit; design now for high-risk compliance to absorb the deadline without rework.
- **Scope creep** — line ministries will ask for ESPA screening, audit-trail synthesis, state-aid drafting. Counter: hold the line on RRF payment-request + ESPA indicator reporting through the framework signature; sister-products spin only after.

# Go-to-Market Plan

## Champion identification
The RRF champion is the **Special Secretary for RRF** at the Ministry of National Economy & Finance. That seat owns the payment-request submission and feels every press cycle when a tranche slips. The Special Secretary has a small technocratic staff and discretion over direct-award procurement at the pilot tier. Secondary RRF champions: the Deputy Minister of National Economy & Finance with the RRF portfolio (political cover), and the **GS for Public Investments** (operational backstop AND the primary buyer for the ESPA bridge).

The cohesion onramp begins inside the RRF pilot: from week one we brief the GS Public Investments office as the "second-cycle owner," so that by the time the RRF instrument winds down the relationship is warm, the audit-trail extension is procured, and the ESPA framework conversation is the natural next step rather than a cold pitch. We do not start with line ministries — they are users, not champions, and starting there fragments the buy.

Introduction route: two warm paths in parallel — (i) a former Special-Secretariat colleague of the founder still inside the building, and (ii) an Athens-based EU-funds partner at a Big-4 firm who routinely briefs both the Special Secretary and the GS Public Investments and respects a tool that makes their work cleaner rather than substitutes for it.

## First-meeting choreography
Thirty minutes, no deck for the first ten. Open with the buyer hook in one sentence: *"We'll show you, on real Q2-2026 milestones, the agent producing a Commission-ready evidence pack in 4 hours that your team currently produces in 3 weeks — and the same loop targeting ESPA indicators by year-end so the work survives 2026-08-31."* Then live demo on a publicly-known Q1-2026 milestone — gazetted law, reform-type, easy to source. Walk through one citation chain end-to-end so the Special Secretary sees the audit trail with their own eyes. Last ten minutes: pilot proposal, named contracting officer, draft scope. Close with one explicit line on the bridge: "We are not asking you to fund a tool that dies with RRF; we are asking you to fund the operational reference that becomes infrastructure for ESPA and the successor."

What we do not do in the first meeting: ask for OPS write-access, name a line ministry by name in front of the Special Secretary, or quote a framework price. Pilot first, framework on cycle two, ESPA bridge on cycle three.

## Pilot offer
- **Scope**: two line ministries, one tranche cycle (six months). Recommended pair: Development (RRF-heavy, friendly to tech) + Environment & Energy (high-visibility milestones).
- **Budget**: €45k–€60k απευθείας ανάθεση, under the standard threshold so no open call.
- **Deliverable**: agent-produced evidence pack + fulfilment summary for every in-scope milestone with an upcoming deadline; side-by-side comparison against the team's manual pack; written replay note from a Special-Secretariat analyst; one-page ESPA-indicator retargeting demo using the same loop on a sample regional OP indicator.
- **Success criterion**: ≥80% of milestone summaries accepted into the submission with edits only (not rewrites); analyst time per milestone down by ≥70%; ESPA retargeting demo signed off by GS Public Investments before pilot end.

## Cohesion-funds onramp (the bridge in GTM)
The pilot is engineered to seed the ESPA framework procurement, not just to bank a €45-60k reference. Three explicit onramp moves run inside the six-month pilot:

1. **Month 1**: GS Public Investments briefing — introduce the loop, agree on which ESPA 2021-2027 OP and which indicator subset will be the bridge demo. Get the GS office to nominate one Managing Authority counterpart for the retargeting exercise.
2. **Month 3**: deliver the ESPA-indicator retargeting demo on real Q2-2026 indicator data alongside the RRF pilot output. This is the artefact that converts the RRF pilot into an ESPA framework conversation.
3. **Month 5**: pre-market consultation with GS Public Investments on a 2027 framework spec. Scope it narrowly enough that the Greek-language + audit-trail-by-design + RRF-cycle reference combination makes us the only credible bidder, and structure the framework as multi-year (2027-2029) with an optional extension into the cohesion-funds successor instrument once that programming is published.

In parallel: a quarterly cohesion-funds programming watch — monitor Commission communications on the 2028-2034 MFF, ECA opinions on the next instrument design, Council positions — and publish a short "cohesion onramp memo" each quarter to keep the GS Public Investments office reading us as the reference vendor before the procurement window opens.

## Expansion path
Cycle two (six months after pilot signature): all thirteen line ministries on the residual RRF cycle plus the ESPA framework signature with GS Public Investments. €350-500k/yr framework, multi-year. Then country two on the cohesion bridge — Cyprus is the natural first export: similar legal substrate, small national plan, English-Greek bilingual administration, GS Public Investments equivalent reachable on a single warm intro.

By 2028 the offer evolves into the post-2027 cohesion successor product: same loop, new template, deployed into the new instrument across the original Greek footprint and 3+ country exports. By then the audit-trail synthesis sister-product (originally a parked development idea) becomes a clean follow-on sold into the same buyer.

## Procurement vehicle
- **RRF pilot**: direct-award (απευθείας ανάθεση) under the €60k threshold, contracted via the Ministry of National Economy & Finance procurement office. Standard one-page scope, six-month term, single fixed-price payment on delivery.
- **ESPA bridge framework**: open competition under standard public-procurement law, scoped narrowly enough that we are the only credible bidder (bilingual Commission-register drafting + audit-trail-by-design + RRF-cycle reference from the pilot + ESPA-indicator retargeting demo). - **Cohesion successor (2028+)**: procurement route depends on the instrument's national implementing law, not yet drafted. We position via the GS Public Investments framework relationship to be the named technical-assistance vendor when the spec emerges.

## PR/comms strategy
Quiet for the first twelve months. No press release on signature, no LinkedIn victory lap on the pilot. The Special Secretariat must be able to claim the work as theirs — the political win is *Greek tranche cleared on time*, not *startup ships agent*. Our public footprint during the pilot is one blog post on Greek-language evidence extraction, technical in tone, no minister mentioned.

After framework signature: one careful joint communication with the Ministry on the public-facing transparency portal, positioning the agent as infrastructure of the Greek EU-funds apparatus — RRF and ESPA, not RRF alone — rather than a third-party product. That framing — *Greece built the agentic reporting layer the Commission doesn't have, and it works across instruments* — is the one that opens the next six cohesion countries and positions Tranchewire as the reference vendor when the 2028-2034 MFF programming concludes.
